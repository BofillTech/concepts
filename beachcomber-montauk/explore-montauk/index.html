Explore Montauk

Out the door, into the End.

Beaches, golf, the iconic Lighthouse, parks, museums, art galleries, sailing, fishing, wineries — all minutes from your suite.

The End of Long Island

The Beachcomber sits on a quiet stretch of Old Montauk Highway — close enough to walk or bike into town, but far enough from the traffic to feel like its own neighborhood. Whatever Montauk you're after — windswept and wild, polished and refined, family-friendly and affordable — it's a short ride away.

Downtown Montauk
Spot — 01

Hither Hills State Park.

Five minutes down the road, Hither Hills sprawls across nearly two miles of oceanfront — open dune trails, rolling sand, and some of the best protected beach access on the East End. A perfect first stop for guests who want the beach big and uncrowded.

5 minutes from the Beachcomber
Spot — 02

Montauk Downs State Park Golf.

A scenic, public 18-hole course set in the hills above town, and one of the best public courses anywhere on Long Island. Bring your clubs — it's a 10-minute drive, and tee times are easier to land than the private alternatives.

10 minutes from the Beachcomber
Spot — 03

The iconic Montauk Lighthouse.

The easternmost tip of Long Island, a few miles past town — and the postcard image of Montauk for over two centuries. Climb the tower for the long view, walk the rocky shoreline, or visit the small museum at the base.

A few miles east of town
Spot — 04

Downtown Montauk.

You can walk it or bike it — the Beachcomber sits close enough to town that you don't have to plan a trip just to grab dinner. Local dining and shopping, parks, museums, art galleries, and the docks for charter fishing and sailing are all within easy reach.

Walk or bike — minutes from the property
And A Whole Lot More

A few more ways to spend the day.

From the water to the vineyards, Montauk has more than enough to fill a long stay.

— 01

Sailing & Fishing

Charter a half-day or full-day trip out of Montauk Harbor — one of the best sportfishing ports on the Atlantic.

— 02

Wineries

The North Fork's wineries are a scenic afternoon drive away — perfect for a tasting and a long lunch.

— 03

Parks & Trails

Beyond Hither Hills, you'll find Camp Hero, Shadmoor, and a network of preserves with hiking and biking.

— 04

Museums & Art

Local museums and art galleries — the East End has a quiet but serious art scene worth a half-day.

— 05

Craft Fairs

Seasonal craft fairs pop up throughout the summer — check the Chamber of Commerce calendar.

— 06

The Beach, Again

Sometimes the best plan is no plan. Beach chair, umbrella, book, repeat.
