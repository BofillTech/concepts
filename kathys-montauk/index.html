About Montauk

The untamed tip of Long Island — where every sunset is a standing ovation.

The Last Wild Place on the East End

Montauk sits at the very eastern tip of New York's South Fork — a place where the manicured Hamptons give way to something wilder and more honest. It's a working fishing village that happens to have some of the finest beaches on the Atlantic seaboard, a 228-year-old lighthouse you can climb, and a summer energy that's both thrilling and unhurried.

The surf culture here is real. Ditch Plains — just down the road from East Lake Villa — is considered one of the best longboard breaks on the East Coast, and you'll find everyone from first-timers to seasoned riders making the pilgrimage every summer morning. Meanwhile, the Atlantic-facing bluffs at Camp Hero offer some of the most dramatic coastal scenery in New York State.

Montauk's dining scene has quietly elevated itself over the past decade. Duryea's Lobster Deck at sunset is almost a religious experience, and Surf Lodge draws a crowd that could have flown in from Ibiza. But the village also still has the clam shacks and fish-and-chips spots that have been there for 50 years, and those are just as good.

The train from Penn Station gets you here in about 3.5 hours — no traffic, just watching the Hamptons pass by the window. Most guests arrive on a Friday afternoon and immediately understand why people return every summer for decades.

Calm, sheltered waters ideal for kayaking, paddleboarding, and swimming. Minutes from East Lake Villa.

🎣

Deep-Sea Fishing

Charter boats depart from Montauk Harbor daily. The striped bass and tuna fishing here is world-class.

Hither Hills State Park

3,000 acres of trails, dunes, and one of the most beautiful campground-to-beach walks in the northeast.
