Welcome

A landmark on Northern Boulevard, and a well-kept Long Island secret.

The East Norwich Inn has stood quietly along Route 25A for decades — a half-timbered Tudor set back from the road, flanked by oaks, with a brick tower that locals have used as a landmark long before GPS did. It's the kind of place that neither shouts for your attention nor needs to.

"A proper inn — warm, genuine, and refreshingly free of the corporate gloss."

Inside, wood-paneled corridors, topiary-lined hallways, and generously sized rooms set the tone. Outside, you're three minutes from the Oyster Bay waterfront, five from Sagamore Hill, and a comfortable half-hour from Manhattan when work or theatre calls.

The North Shore

At the center of the Gold Coast.

East Norwich sits at the quiet crossroads of Long Island's North Shore — the historic stretch of grand estates, harbor towns, and tree-lined parkways that F. Scott Fitzgerald borrowed for Gatsby's East and West Egg. We are five minutes from Oyster Bay Harbor, ten from the vineyards, and a comfortable half-hour from the city.
