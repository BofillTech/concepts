How to get here.

Three airports. Four good ways across the water. One short ride up the hill. We'll walk you through every step — no guesswork, no panicked WhatsApps from the ferry dock.

The short version

Fly into Tortola (EIS) if you can — it's the most direct. Otherwise fly into St. Thomas (STT) and take a ferry or water taxi. Either way, aim to land before 3 PM local time.

— Critical timing

When should I aim to land?

The last ferry to Jost is real — and it doesn't wait. These are the numbers that matter.

✓

Ideal arrival

Land by 1:00 PM local on your arrival day. This gives you a comfortable cushion for customs, taxi, and ferry — with no stress.

!

Latest safe arrival (STT)

Land by 3:00 PM. Last Aquatic ferry from Red Hook is around 5:00 PM, and the last New Horizon to JVD leaves West End around 6:00 PM.

!

Latest safe arrival (EIS)

Land by 4:00 PM if you're using the New Horizon ferry from West End. Water taxi is more forgiving — we can often arrange later crossings.

✕

Land after the last ferry?

Stay the night in Tortola or St. Thomas and cross in the morning. Don't try to push it — overnight crossings are expensive and rarely worth it.

◷

Check in early

Ferry check-in is 30–45 minutes before departure, and all BVI/USVI crossings are international — you'll clear customs at West End.

→

Departing JVD

For early-morning departures, book your island taxi at least a day ahead. WhatsApp works best with our local drivers.

— Once you're on Jost

The last mile: Great Harbour to White Bay.

The ferry drops you at Great Harbour. From there, it's a 5–7 minute taxi ride over the hill to our property at White Bay — or about a 15–20 minute walk if you're traveling light. Taxis are always waiting when the ferry rolls in. Save a few numbers to your phone before you fly.
