forty years on whitmarsh lane —

A family,
a lane, a life.

Linda & Chuck Krumel built Courtney's Place as a legacy to their daughter Courtney. This is how a New York art major and a Virginia singer-songwriter wound up on the highest point in Key West.

The lane

Once it was called Cat Alley.

In the early 1950s it was renamed Whitmarsh Lane — after Mary Whitmarsh, one of Hemingway's best friends. She owned one of our cottages.

Our guest quarters were created out of Key West cottages and homes well over a hundred years old. We happen to sit on the highest point on the Island — eighteen feet above sea level. Our neighbourhood is one of authenticity and has seen little change over the last fifty years. It's still made up of some of the original descendants of the Island — Conchs, Bahamians, dogs, cats, birds.

Legend has it that years ago during Key West's heyday as a shipwrecking town, pirates would come up to this area and bury their gold and treasures stolen from the cargo ships that lay wrecked on the Atlantic coral reefs.

Several years back, one of our neighbours, while digging a swimming pool, found $10,000 in gold coins.

(No shovels, please.)

Linda & Chuck

An art major and a singer-songwriter.

Linda came to Key West on vacation from the cold north. Chuck came from Virginia by way of music. They fell in love with this island first, and then with each other in the early '70s.

The '70s were the heyday of our era in Key West. The island hadn't been discovered by the world yet. It was a haven for artists, writers, smugglers, shrimpers, hippies, gays, and corruption — houses were selling for a few thousand dollars apiece. We got our master's degrees in renovating conch houses, and working at Capt. Tony's Saloon.

Linda ran the saloon. Chuck was a singer/songwriter. We partied with the best.

We got our master's degrees
renovating Conch houses.

Along came the '80s, and Courtney was born. Career change — there was nowhere to buy clothes for her, so we opened a children's department store, A World for Children, on Duval Street.

During the late '80s, Key West was making big changes from a fishing village to a tourist destination. A close friend from the '70s, Shel Silverstein, suggested we open a guesthouse with a restaurant — he loved Linda's cooking. Courtney's Place was born.

A few markers in the sand

The milestones.

1950s

Cat Alley becomes Whitmarsh Lane

The lane is renamed for Mary Whitmarsh, Hemingway's friend. She owns one of the cottages that's now part of Courtney's Place.

1970s

Linda & Chuck arrive — separately

Linda from New York, Chuck from Virginia. They meet, fall in love, and Linda runs Capt. Tony's Saloon while Chuck plays music.

1980s

Courtney is born

They open A World for Children on Duval Street — the island's first dedicated children's clothing store.

Today

Courtney runs the place

Courtney owns and operates Courtney's Place with the help of family. Staff is family. The cast of regulars hasn't changed all that much.
