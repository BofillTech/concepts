The Big Three

Right out the door.

The three places people remember when they get home. Each one is a short drive from the motel — closer than most guests expect.

8 min · 6 mi south

State Park · Beach

Bahia Honda State Park

The clearest water in the Keys, framed by palms and the rusted skeleton of the old railroad bridge. Two and a half miles of beach across three sections (Sandspur, Calusa, Loggerhead), a snorkel reef just offshore, a marina that rents kayaks and standup paddleboards, and a campground if you ever want to extend the stay. Pack a cooler in the morning and stay through sunset — the bridge view is worth it.

Open daily 8 AM · Last entry one hour before sunset

In the neighborhood

Wildlife Refuge

National Key Deer Refuge

Big Pine Key is the heart of the refuge. The deer are the size of a labrador, completely native to these islands, and federally endangered. They wander the neighborhood streets at dawn and dusk — especially along Key Deer Boulevard and Watson Boulevard. Drive 25 mph, leave your snacks in the room, and you'll see a handful before dinner.

Visitor Center on Key Deer Blvd

5 min · 2.5 mi

Roadside Legend

The No Name Pub

Down a road you'd swear leads nowhere, on the way to No Name Key — one of the most loved roadside pubs in the country. Dollar bills cover every inch of the walls (and the ceiling). The pizza is legendary. The Key Lime pie is the real thing. You'll talk about dinner here for years.

Daily 11 AM – 9 PM · Cash & cards

On the Water

Looe Key Reef is right here.

Five miles offshore sits Looe Key, one of the most spectacular sections of the third-largest barrier reef in the world. Multiple dive shops in Big Pine and Ramrod Key (just north) run morning snorkel and dive trips out to the reef — about 30 minutes by boat. You'll see angelfish, parrotfish, barracuda, sea turtles, and the occasional reef shark gliding through.

If you brought the boat, even better — we've got the parking for it.

The Backcountry

Bonefish, tarpon, and quiet water.

The flats off Big Pine and No Name Key are some of the best skinny-water fishing in the world. Tarpon in the spring, bonefish year-round, permit if you're lucky and patient. Half a dozen local guides run charters from Big Pine and Summerland Key (next island up).

Not into fishing? Same flats are perfect for kayaking — rent a sit-on-top in town and paddle the mangrove channels for a morning.

North & South

Two cities within reach.

From MM 30, Marathon is 30 miles north and Key West is 30 miles south. Both are easy day trips — or easy nights out and back.

30 mi north

Day Trip

Marathon

The middle Keys hub: the Turtle Hospital (real sea turtle rehabilitation, open for tours), the Dolphin Research Center, Sombrero Beach (a real public beach with a long sandy stretch), and the eastern landing of the 7 Mile Bridge. Plenty of seafood spots — Keys Fisheries, Sunset Grille, the Stuffed Pig for breakfast. About 40 minutes by car.

~40 min drive on US-1

30 mi south

Day Trip

Key West

Duval Street, Mallory Square at sunset, the Hemingway House and its six-toed cats, the Southernmost Point buoy, the Truman Little White House. Sloppy Joe's. Blue Heaven. The Green Parrot. About 45 minutes by car, with parking at the public garages (Old Town Garage is the move) so you don't circle for an hour.

~45 min drive on US-1

More to See

A few local notes.

Less famous, equally worth your time.

The Blue Hole

A freshwater pond on Key Deer Blvd — rare in the Keys — with resident alligators, turtles, and herons. Strange and worth seeing.

5 min

7 Mile Bridge

The iconic span between Marathon and the Lower Keys. Sunrise from the Pigeon Key end is spectacular.

12 min

Bahia Honda Bridge

The old railroad bridge ruins inside Bahia Honda State Park. Worth the walk to the top for the view.

8 min

Looe Key Reef

Snorkel and dive trips run daily from Big Pine & Ramrod Key. ~30 min by boat.

Off MM 27

Bat Tower · Sugarloaf Key

A weird piece of Keys history — a 1929 tower built to attract bats to fight mosquitoes (it didn't work). 15 min south.

15 min

Coupon Bight Aquatic Preserve

Protected seagrass flats just south of Big Pine — kayak it at dawn for the best chance at seeing rays and small sharks.

10 min
