Around Big Pine Key

Step Out the Door

Bahia Honda is eight minutes away.

The clearest water in the Keys, white sand framed by palms, and a snorkel reef just offshore. It's a state park most people drive hours to reach. From here, it's the next exit.

Pack a cooler in the morning, be back by sunset. Or stay for the sunset — the bridge view is worth it.

Local Wildlife

Home of the Key Deer.

Big Pine Key is one of the only places on earth you'll see them — miniature deer, about the size of a labrador, native to these islands and protected as a federally endangered species. They wander right through the neighborhood at dusk.

Drive slow on the side roads, leave the snacks in the room, and you'll spot a few before dinner.

After Dark

The No Name Pub.

Five minutes from the motel, down a road you'd swear leads nowhere, sits one of the most loved roadside pubs in the Keys. Dollar bills cover the walls. The pizza is legendary. The Key Lime pie is the real thing.

It's the kind of dinner spot you tell your friends about for years afterward.
