Our Corner of Wisconsin

The Best-Kept Secret
on Lake Michigan

Door County is a 75-mile thumb of land jutting into Lake Michigan, home to ten state parks, eleven lighthouses, and hundreds of miles of shoreline. Baileys Harbor sits on the quiet, eastern side of the peninsula — away from the summer crowds but close to everything that makes Door County special.

From our resort you can walk to the Ridges Sanctuary, the old Birdcage Lighthouse, and Toft Point. Within 15 minutes you have access to cherry orchards, world-class fishing, sailing, kayaking, farm-to-table dining, summer theater, and enough festivals to fill any calendar.

Family Time · February 2018

Wisconsin Highway 42 to Baileys Harbor

It’s true that the fastest way to Baileys Harbor is Highway 57, but we take Highway 42. It’s only a four-mile difference but it takes two hours longer — and it’s time well spent. Door County Coffee in Carlsville, a go-kart race at Egg Harbor Fun Park, a bottle of Carmen’s Last Dance from Harbor Ridge Winery… by the time we pull into Baileys Harbor, we are firmly in vacation mode.

Dining · April 2017

Jacksonport – Door County Farmers Market at Lakeside Park

The Jacksonport Farmers Market runs Tuesdays 9am–1pm from mid-May through late October at Lakeside Park on Route 57. Mixed lettuce, fingerling potatoes, honey, handmade candy, Big Band music from Birch Creek students — and a baguette that looked amazing. Fresh produce from the lake shore, plus easy access to a small beach and picnic tables right on Lake Michigan.

Door County · January 2017

Search for the Best Cherry Pie

Cherries are synonymous with Door County. In August, cherry tree branches are dotted red with ripe fruit. After chasing the perfect pie from Falcon Orchard to Seaquist, Sweetie Pies, and Grandma Tommy’s, one thing is certain: every pie we found was good — and the search itself is half the fun of a Door County summer.
