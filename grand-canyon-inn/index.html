I.The Inn

A family-run inn in Grand Canyon country.

Three generations. One stretch of highway. A hundred and one rooms between the Kaibab and the Coconino.

Our story begins in the early 1950s, when a hardworking family from Canada moved south and built the very first Grand Canyon Inn and Restaurant — a small roadside outfit between Red Lake and the Canyon, with a service station out front and the dust of Arizona settling over everything.

In 1977 the family moved to Valle and built the Grand Canyon Motel. Four years later, the first thirty-three rooms of the present Inn went up. Two new wings and an outdoor pool followed in the mid-nineties. Today the Inn has grown to a hundred and one rooms — still independently owned, still focused on what matters out here: a clean bed, a hot meal, and a short drive to the rim.

III.Eat & Drink

Restaurant. Saloon. Cold beer, hot pizza, a long bar to lean on.

Open daily for breakfast, lunch and dinner — steaks, burgers, salads, charbroiled chicken, quesadillas, and the made-with-love pizza that gives travelers second thoughts about pushing on to Williams.

The Restaurant

An alternating seasonal menu with something for everyone.

Large seating capacity, family atmosphere. Start the day with a hearty breakfast or a nutritious light bite — and finish with steaks, burgers, salads, sandwiches, quesadillas, or a hand-built pizza.

The Bar

Spirits, local craft beer.

A great collection of distilled spirits and a rotating tap of local craft beers — including specialty beers from Grand Canyon Brewery — poured in a Southwestern room of warm wood and easy company.

The Lounge

A relaxed Southwestern room.

A friendly, vibrant gathering place — made-to-order drinks from attentive bartenders, large-screen televisions for the game, and the kind of unhurried evening you can only find at a family-run inn.
