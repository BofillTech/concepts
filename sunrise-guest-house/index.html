i. Welcome

One of Montauk's best-kept secrets, tucked away by the sea.

Stacey and John have run the place since 1995 — a four-room beach house on a quiet bend of Old Montauk Highway, with a porch that watches the Atlantic and a kitchen that smells of homemade quiche by 8 a.m.

You'll find no lobby here, no concierge desk, no marble. What you'll find instead is a house — warm, lived-in, and laid out to make you feel like you've finally stopped traveling.

A travelers' paradise since 1995. Romantic, laid back — even in peak season, there are very few people on our beach.

ii. The Rooms

Four suites.
Three with the ocean.

Each suite is roughly three hundred square feet — large enough to live in for a week, small enough to feel like yours. Three of the four face the water; all four have ensuite baths, fireplaces, flat-screens, and the sound of the surf if you crack a window at night.

The Front Suite

Wide windows facing the Atlantic and the morning sun. A sleigh bed, a sliding door to the porch, and the sort of quiet you forgot existed.

The Fireplace Suite

Two rooms divided by a half-wall: a bed by the windows, a sitting area by the fireplace. Open the slider and you'll hear the surf above the firelight.

Set back from the road, with the same view of the water and a touch more quiet. Antique furnishings, a generous bath, and a porch step from the door.

The Tucked-In Suite

The hideaway — set back from the highway and as quiet as the house gets. The view is of the garden, but you'll still hear the surf at night.

iii. Mornings

Breakfast on the porch.

Some mornings, Stacey makes a quiche. Some mornings, it's biscotti, or muffins still warm from the oven. There's always coffee, and the binoculars on the windowsill, in case the whales pass.

You can take a plate out to the porch and watch the sun climb out of the Atlantic. Most guests do.

iv. The Beach

A beach that feels like yours.

Cross the road. There's a path through the dunes, a stretch of sand that runs for miles in either direction, and almost no one on it. We keep beach chairs, umbrellas, a cooler, and bikes — take what you need.

Find your way to us.
