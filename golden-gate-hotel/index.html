01The Hotel

Where San Francisco's bygone era still holds court.

Built in 1913, our façade is one of the most distinctive along Bush Street. Inside, twenty-three guest rooms — each with totally different design and décor.

Choose from rooms with private en-suite bathrooms, many featuring the original claw-foot tubs, or our European-style rooms with separate, shared bathrooms down the hall. Throughout, you'll find antiques, decorative artwork, and details from a city that hasn't quite forgotten itself.

02Our Story

One hundred and thirteen years of warmth and originality.

Edwardian-inspired furnishings. Bay windows that catch the morning. The faint, distinctive ring of a cable car climbing Bush Street. Every detail at Golden Gate Hotel is a small love letter to the San Francisco of another century.

"Floral elements, beautifully maintained wood furniture, a vintage bird-cage elevator, and an ornate fireplace in the parlor. Twenty-three rooms — and not one repeats itself."

This isn't a hotel that's been polished into anonymity. It's a hotel that has chosen, deliberately, to stay itself.

04Resident in Chief

Meet Skittles.

Tabby Age 3 SF SPCA Alumna

She moved in last December, and she's already made quite the impression on the staff and the guests alike.

A sweet, charming three-year-old tabby adopted from the San Francisco SPCA, Skittles spreads happiness around the lobby with a particular kind of indifference that only cats can pull off. She's a regular ambassador, an occasional inspector of luggage, and quite possibly the reason your stay feels like home.

05Mornings

A rare San Francisco perk.

A complimentary breakfast, served next door.

Crispy bacon, scrambled eggs, freshly baked croissants and baguettes. Wheat and white bread. Cheese slices, Canadian bacon, three cereals, hearty oatmeal — and yogurt for the disciplined among us.

Treat yourself to freshly made waffles, paired with juice, freshly brewed coffee, tea, or hot chocolate. Breakfast is served at our sister property, the Cornell Hotel, just one minute up Bush Street.

07The Address

The crossroads of everywhere in San Francisco.

Up the hill from Union Square. A few blocks from Chinatown. Steps from the Powell/Mason and Powell/Hyde cable-car stops, with Nob Hill at your back and the rest of the city laid out in front of you.

When you stay with us, the best of San Francisco is right out your front door — and in the case of the cable cars, audible from your bay window.
