A Local Guide

The Cape.

Beaches, ferries, day trips, dinners that go long. Here's how locals actually spend a Cape Cod week.

From Dennis Port, with love

A Cape Cod week, real and unhurried.

Dennis Port sits on Cape Cod's south shore, where the water is warm and the towns are quiet. The whole peninsula opens up from here — ferries to the islands, the National Seashore an hour up, and a couple hundred years of seafaring villages in between.

Right Outside the Door

Dennis Port, at a glance.

A south-shore village within the town of Dennis — calm, walkable, family-first. Old wharves, working fishermen, and the warmest swimming water on Cape Cod, no exaggeration.

Quiet shore, warm water, just enough town.

Dennis Port has held onto a small-town feel that other parts of the Cape have started to lose. Old Wharf Road runs the shore, lined with shingled cottages and family-owned places that have been here since before air conditioning was a feature. The Sound is shallow and slow to warm — by July, kids stay in until lips turn purple.

  • 01
    The Sound, not the Atlantic South-facing beaches with calm, warm water — a different Cape than the cold Outer Cape surf.
  • 02
    Walkable in pockets Old Wharf Road, downtown Dennis Port, and the harbor area are all on foot from here.
  • 03
    Cape Cod Rail Trail The 25-mile rail trail runs through Dennis — bike rentals available a few minutes away.
  • 04
    Hyannis & ferries, 20 minutes Both the Steamship Authority and Hy-Line ferries leave from Hyannis to the islands.

Sand, Sound & Surf

The beaches nearby.

You have a private beach right here, but if you want to wander — here are the ones we'd point you to. All within a few minutes by car.

Right Here · 0 min

Our Private Beach

For registered guests only. Calm Sound water, soft sand, and steps from the sundeck. The easiest beach day of your week.

5 min by car

West Dennis Beach

A long, wide, dune-backed beach — one of the largest on the Cape's south shore. Big parking lot, lifeguards, snack bar.

8 min by car

Mayflower Beach

On the bay side of Dennis. Famous for its tidal flats — the water goes out half a mile at low tide. Magical for kids.

10 min by car

Chapin Beach

Dennis's wild and quiet bay-side beach — long stretches of sand, easy parking, and one of the best Cape sunsets you'll see.

15 min by car

Craigville Beach

Centerville's classic family beach — warm, calm, and walkable from a small village core. A nice change of scenery.

25 min by car

Nauset Beach

Outer Cape, Atlantic side. Big surf, big sky, big crowds in August. Worth the drive at least once. Bring a sweater.

Half-Day & Full-Day

The day trips worth taking.

Cape Cod is small, but it does hold three islands and one of the country's best national seashores. These are the trips we tell guests to actually plan for.

From Hyannis · ferry

Nantucket

Cobblestone streets, gray-shingled mansions, beaches that go on. The fast ferry from Hyannis is an hour each way.
From Hyannis · ferry

Martha's Vineyard

Six different towns, each its own character. Edgartown for charm, Aquinnah for cliffs, Oak Bluffs for the gingerbread cottages.
1 hr drive

Provincetown

The tip of the Cape. Galleries, drag shows, dunes, whale-watching boats out of MacMillan Pier. Stay for the sunset over the bay.
45 min drive

National Seashore

Forty miles of protected shoreline. Marconi Beach, Coast Guard Beach, Race Point. The Cape's wildest face.
35 min drive

Chatham

Polished, quintessential Cape Cod. Main Street shopping, the Chatham Lighthouse, and the seal-spotting boat from Stage Harbor.
15 min drive

Hyannis & the Kennedys

The JFK Hyannis Museum, Main Street, the harbor, and the Kennedy Compound view from Veterans Park.

When to Come

The Cape, by season.

We're open all year. Each season has its own version of Cape Cod — here's what to expect.

Late Spring
May · June

Towns waking up, restaurants reopening, lilacs blooming. Water still cold but air gets warm fast. The locals' favorite time before the crowds.

Summer
July · August

Cape Cod at full volume. Warmest swimming, longest days, busiest streets. Book early, eat early, hit the beach before 10am.

Early Fall
Sept · Oct

Locals call it the best Cape weeks of the year. Water still warm, restaurants quiet, light goes golden by 6pm. Highly recommend.

Off-Season
Nov – April

Sweater-weather Cape. Empty beaches, woodsmoke, cozy local-only restaurants. Different kind of trip — quiet and contemplative.
