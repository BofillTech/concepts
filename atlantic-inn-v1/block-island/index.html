Where We Are

An island unto itself.

Twelve square miles of cliffs, harbors, beaches, and rolling moors — a National Wildlife Refuge, a string of historic lighthouses, and an unhurried rhythm you can feel the moment you step off the ferry.

The Atlantic sits at the highest point above Old Harbor, on the island's east side — minutes from town, the beaches, the bluffs, and everything Block Island has to offer.

Things To Do

Crescent Beach

1.7 mi · 5 min drive

Three miles of soft sand stretching along the east side of the island — the closest beach to the Inn and an easy walk down the hill on a clear morning.

Mohegan Bluffs

3 mi · 8 min drive

Dramatic clay cliffs rising 150 feet above the Atlantic. A wooden staircase leads down to the beach below — one of the most photographed views on the island.

Old Harbor

0.5 mi · 9 min walk

The heart of New Shoreham — shops, restaurants, galleries, and the ferry dock. Just down the hill from the Inn.

Great Salt Pond

2 mi · 6 min drive

A 700-acre tidal pond on the west side of the island, ringed by marinas and seafood shacks. Catch the sunset from Dead Eye Dick's.

North Lighthouse

5 mi · 15 min drive

A historic granite lighthouse built in 1867, marking the northern tip of Block Island. Reachable by a scenic walk along Sandy Point.

Clayhead Trail

3.5 mi · 10 min drive

Winding nature trails along the bluffs of the island's northeast corner — the famous "Maze" of footpaths through bayberry and beach plum.

Ballard's Beach

0.8 mi · 10 min walk

A wide, lively beach with chaise lounges, umbrellas, live music, and the iconic Ballard's Inn just steps from the ferry.

Block Island Historical Society Museum

1 mi · 11 min walk

A nineteenth-century farmhouse tracing the island's farming, fishing, and lifesaving heritage. Worth an hour on a quiet afternoon.

Getting Here

Three ferries, a small plane, and a quiet welcome.

Block Island is reached by ferry from Point Judith and Newport, Rhode Island, and from New London, Connecticut. A small airport on the island serves connecting flights from Westerly. Cars are allowed but discouraged — most guests rent a bike or moped or simply walk.

From the ferry dock to the Inn is about a five-minute drive up the hill, or a fifteen-minute walk on a pleasant day.
