A Word from Tim

Find yourself & lose your worries in the breeze.

On a gentle rise above Nantucket Sound, The Oceanside is a quiet community of beachfront condominiums and cottages. Each unit is owned and decorated by a family who loves Cape Cod — and who wants to share that love with you.

Wake up to ocean breezes. Spend mornings poolside and afternoons walking the sand. Grill dinner on the porch. The Cape is right outside your door — picturesque villages, lighthouses, fishing piers, mini golf, and a hundred small pleasures.

The Pool & The Sound

Two ways to swim. One impossible-to-leave view.

The heated, fresh-water pool sits at the heart of the property, open Memorial Day through Labor Day. Beyond the gate, a short barefoot walk takes you to the warm, calm waters of Nantucket Sound — the kind of beach that's made for kids learning to swim and parents reading a paperback in a low chair.

  • Heated freshwater pool
  • Steps to Nantucket Sound
  • Gas grills & outdoor seating
  • Free WiFi throughout
  • Free on-site parking
  • Children of all ages welcome

The Stays

Thirty-eight units. No two alike.

Every unit is owned and decorated by a different Cape Cod family. Each sleeps four with two separate private rooms — some with private porches and ocean views. All include kitchenettes, cable, free WiFi, and individually-controlled AC and heat.

Around the Cape

A vacation wonderland
just outside the gate.

Beaches & Surf

Swim, walk, and play on the warm sand of Nantucket Sound. Sea Street Beach is half a mile away.

Golf & Mini Golf

From championship courses to Susan's Garden Mini Golf next door — bring the clubs and the kids.

Lighthouses

Discover historic lighthouses along the Cape's coastline — Nobska, Highland, and beyond.

Whale Watching

Day trips from Hyannis and Provincetown to see humpbacks, dolphins, and minke whales offshore.

Islands

Ferry excursions to Nantucket and Martha's Vineyard make for an unforgettable day off-Cape.

Fine Dining

Lobster shacks, raw bars, and white-tablecloth dining rooms — the Cape eats well, simply.
