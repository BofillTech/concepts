A note from Anne

A calm, organized partner for one of life’s biggest decisions.

After 25 years as a guidance counselor at G.W. Hewlett High School, I opened my advising practice in 2014 with a simple goal: give every family the kind of personal, thoughtful guidance that turns a stressful process into an exciting one.

Parents and students often tell me the same thing — that the college process feels like trying to read a map in the dark. My job is to turn on the light. Together, we’ll clarify what you’re looking for, build a thoughtful list, write essays you’re proud of, and make confident decisions at every fork in the road.

The Process

Four phases. One thoughtful journey.

No two students arrive at the same college the same way. My work follows a deliberate, four-phase framework — flexible enough to fit your story, structured enough to keep every deadline on the rails.

  1. I

    Self‑Assessment

    We start with reflection. What do you want to study? How far from home? Urban, suburban, or rural? Academic fit, social fit, financial fit, and personal fit all matter — and they all begin with honest conversation.

  2. II

    College Exploration

    Together we build a thoughtful list of colleges worth researching — informed by your goals, my college visits, information workshops, and on‑the‑ground knowledge of how admissions actually works today.

  3. III

    Application Assistance

    Hands‑on help with applications, essay drafting and feedback, interview preparation, and a clear deadline plan. The goal isn’t just submitting — it’s submitting your best.

  4. IV

    Decisions & Next Steps

    When letters arrive — acceptances, deferrals, or waitlists — we strategize together. Once all your decisions are in, I’ll help you weigh the options and choose the school that’s truly right for you.
