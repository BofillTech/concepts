Ogunquit, Maine

Everything You Need, Steps Away

From a 3-mile sandy beach to the renowned Marginal Way cliff walk, Ogunquit offers some of New England’s finest coastal experiences — and most of it is walkable from Terrace By The Sea.

Beach

Ogunquit Beach

Three miles of pristine white sand — consistently rated one of America’s best beaches. Family-friendly with calm river-side waters and open Atlantic surf.

📍 2 min walk from our front door

Walking Path

The Marginal Way

A breathtaking 1.5-mile cliff walk along the rocky Atlantic coastline. One of Maine’s most celebrated scenic walks — best at sunrise or sunset.

📍 5 min walk

Village & Harbor

Perkins Cove

A charming lobstering village at the end of the Marginal Way, filled with galleries, boutiques, and the finest seafood restaurants in Southern Maine.

📍 15 min walk via Marginal Way

Arts & Culture

Ogunquit Playhouse

America’s Foremost Summer Theatre — a national landmark offering world-class productions June through October in a beautiful 750-seat venue.

📍 10 min walk

Museum

Ogunquit Museum of American Art

A stunning museum overlooking Perkins Cove with an exceptional collection of American art in a breathtaking coastal setting. Open June–November.

📍 20 min walk

Recreation

Kayaking & Whale Watching

Rent kayaks on the Ogunquit River, book a whale watching cruise from Perkins Cove, or arrange deep-sea fishing with local captains.

📍 Perkins Cove — 15 min walk

Local Dining

Where to Eat & Drink

Ogunquit’s restaurant scene punches well above its size — world-class seafood, farm-to-table, and everything in between.

Barnacle Billy’s

Seafood

Legendary lobster and seafood on the water at Perkins Cove. An Ogunquit institution since 1961.

MC Perkins Cove

Fine Dining

Upscale coastal cuisine from James Beard Award-winning chef Mark Gaier, with sweeping harbor views.

The Front Porch

American & Piano Bar

A beloved Ogunquit staple with live piano entertainment, craft cocktails, and crowd-pleasing American fare.

98 Provence

French Bistro

Authentic French bistro cuisine in a warm, intimate setting — one of the region’s most acclaimed restaurants.

A special place on the Maine coast since 1920. Directly overlooking Ogunquit Beach.
