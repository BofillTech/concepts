Amagansett & the South Fork

Quiet, down-to-earth charm.

Amagansett takes its name from the Montaukett word for “place of good water.” It’s 6.6 square miles between East Hampton and Montauk — the section known as Napeague — with the Atlantic to the south and Gardiner’s Bay to the north. The double dunes and walking dunes are part of what makes this stretch of the South Fork feel different.

The village has drawn a discerning summer crowd for generations, thanks to the natural beauty and that quiet, down-to-earth charm. Expect relaxed-but-upscale restaurants, gourmet shops, boutique storefronts, and historic houses tucked everywhere you look.

Outdoors

What to do, just outside the door.

01

Walk or Run the Beach

Steps from the door

Our private beach stretches in both directions — perfect for a morning walk or sunset jog. Beach chairs and umbrellas are waiting.

02

The Walking Dunes

Napeague State Park

One of the South Fork’s most distinctive landscapes — slowly migrating dunes you can actually hike. Trailheads minutes from the property.

03

Windsurf the Bay

Napeague Harbor

Napeague Harbor is across the road — protected, breezy, and known among locals as one of the best windsurfing spots on the East End.

04

Kayak the Harbor

Napeague Harbor

Calmer waters than the ocean. Rentals available in town — ideal for a quiet morning paddle.

05

Deep-Sea Fishing

Montauk, 10 min east

Charter boats sail daily from Montauk Harbor for striped bass, fluke, blackfish, and more depending on the season.

06

Montauk Lighthouse

15 min drive east

The oldest lighthouse in New York State, authorized by George Washington in 1792. Tours, a small museum, and trails along the bluffs.

Eat, Drink, Explore

Where the locals go.

01

Morty’s Oyster Bar

Special offers for guests

Oysters, po-boys, and a casual beach-town crowd. Ask about our guest offers when you arrive.

02

Restaurants Within a Walk

~½ mile

Two restaurants serving lunch and dinner are within a short walk. Goldberg’s Bagels is right across the road for takeout breakfast and lunch.

03

East Hampton Village

12 min drive west

Shopping, galleries, gourmet markets, and some of the South Fork’s best dining — without the Main Beach traffic.

04

Amagansett Square

10 min drive west

Boutique shopping, the famous farmers market, and a low-key local-village vibe that’s a refreshing contrast to East Hampton.

05

Museums & Galleries

All summer

Pollock-Krasner House, Guild Hall, LongHouse Reserve, and a calendar of summer arts festivals across the South Fork.

06

Hither Hills State Park

10 min walk east

Hiking trails, fishing access, and the start of the Walking Dunes loop — one of the best beach walks on Long Island.
