Behind the bookshelf

Above us, a bistro called Pompette. Below us, a longer story.

Little Rich Girl lives in the basement of 101 N. Leroux, beneath the bright dining rooms of Pompette. The room is anchored in walnut and dark jewel tones — bookshelves to the ceiling, velvet banquettes, a zinc-wrapped bar lit by brass. Antique mirrors do most of the looking back.

The cocktails read like editions of a private library. Some are dog-eared classics; others were written this week. Either way, they are best served quietly, in the company of someone you trust enough to tell a secret.

The Room

A room built like a secret.

Eight things to know about the space before you find your way down to it.

01 · The Bar

Zinc & Walnut

A long zinc-topped bar fronted in walnut panelling and fluted tile, set against an antique mirrored back. Twelve seats, no reservations at the bar.

02 · The Library

Walls of Stories

Floor-to-ceiling shelves lined with first editions and forgotten favorites. Pull one if you like; nobody is watching except the room itself.

03 · The Booths

Velvet, Low Light

Plush velvet banquettes in deep jewel tones — emerald, garnet, rose. Built for two who want to disappear, or four who want to be overheard.

04 · The PDR

Private Dining, Hidden

A second, smaller library tucks behind a hidden door — a private dining room for six to twelve, open by request only. Ask the hostess.

05 · The Sound

Vinyl, Just Loud Enough

A curated needle-drop program: late-night jazz, slow soul, a little Françoise Hardy. Loud enough to dance to, quiet enough to talk over.

06 · The Light

Brass & Candlelight

Brass library lamps at every table. Beeswax candles on the bar. Nothing fluorescent has ever crossed the threshold and nothing ever will.

Hidden Entrance

You'll know the door when you find it.

  1. Begin upstairs at Pompette. The bistro at 101 N. Leroux. Order something light if you'd like; you'll be back later.
  2. Look for the hallway past the bar. The unmarked door at the end is ours. There is no sign. There never will be.
  3. Down the stairs, into the library. Tell the hostess your name. If we know it, we'll have your table ready.
