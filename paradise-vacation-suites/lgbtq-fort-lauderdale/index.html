A Traveler's Guide

Your LGBTQ+ Guide to Fort Lauderdale & Wilton Manors

How the Greater Fort Lauderdale area, and Wilton Manors in particular, became the second-largest LGBTQ+ destination in the United States — and how to make the most of a visit.

TL;DR

Wilton Manors, Florida, has the second-highest concentration of same-sex households of any city in the United States (per the 2020 US Census) — second only to Provincetown, MA. The city anchors Wilton Drive, a mile of bars, restaurants, and nightlife that has been the cultural heart of LGBTQ+ South Florida for thirty years. Greater Fort Lauderdale has hosted Pride events for over four decades, runs the largest gay rugby tournament in North America, and has been certified by HRC with a perfect 100/100 Municipal Equality Index score every year since 2013.

Why Wilton Manors

The transformation of Wilton Manors from a quiet 1950s Fort Lauderdale suburb into a globally-recognized LGBTQ+ destination is one of the more remarkable urban stories in modern American history. The shift began quietly in the late 1980s as gay and lesbian residents moved into the affordable single-family neighborhoods adjacent to Wilton Drive. By the 1990s, LGBTQ+-owned businesses had begun opening along the Drive itself. By 2010, the city had its first openly gay mayor and majority-LGBTQ+ city commission.

Today, approximately 43% of Wilton Manors households are same-sex couples — a figure unmatched anywhere in the country outside of Provincetown. The city has continuously scored 100/100 on Human Rights Campaign's Municipal Equality Index since 2013, a designation shared by only a handful of US cities.

By the Numbers
Wilton Manors: 1.95 sq mi · ~12,000 residents · 43% same-sex households · HRC Municipal Equality Index 100/100 · Home to the largest Pride parade in southeast Florida.

Stonewall Pride Wilton Manors — The Big One

Stonewall Pride Wilton Manors is the largest Pride event in southeast Florida. Held in mid-June each year on Wilton Drive itself, the parade and accompanying street festival draws an estimated 50,000+ attendees annually. The full Drive shuts down to traffic, the bars open their patios, and the city becomes one continuous celebration from late morning through the night.

"Stonewall Pride isn't a corporate float parade. It's a community event in the city's own commercial corridor — which means everyone you pass is either marching, watching, or stepping out of a bar to wave."

If you're planning a visit around Stonewall Pride, book six months in advance. Lodging fills up by January for the following June. Day-of attendance is free; many bars and venues run ticketed events for pre- and post-parade activities.

Annual LGBTQ+ Events

Stonewall Pride is the largest, but it's not the only event worth planning around.

  • Fort Lauderdale Pride (October) — The original Greater Fort Lauderdale pride event, dating to the 1980s. More compact than Stonewall Pride, with a beach orientation.
  • Sebastian Street Festival (June) — Held on the gay section of Fort Lauderdale Beach.
  • Florida Bears events (year-round) — The bear community is large, organized, and welcoming.
  • Aqua Girl (May, Miami Beach but heavily attended from Wilton Manors) — One of the largest lesbian events on the East Coast.
  • Fort Lauderdale International Gay Rugby Tournament (multiple times yearly) — The largest gay rugby tournament in North America.
  • Drag Brunches (Sundays, year-round) — Hosted by Rosie's, The Manor, and several other venues. Make reservations.

Wilton Drive — The Heart

The roughly one-mile commercial spine of Wilton Manors is where most LGBTQ+ Fort Lauderdale nightlife happens. Highlights include:

  • The Manor Complex — Multi-venue including The Manor (large dance floor), Eden (lounge), Honey (cocktail bar), and a rotating roster of drag, DJ, and live performance.
  • Hunters Nightclub — The Drive's largest dance club. Drag shows nightly, themed events most weekends.
  • Georgie's Alibi Monkey Bar — Multi-room neighborhood bar — pool tables, karaoke, trivia. A reliable midweek option.
  • The New Moon — Lesbian bar (all welcome), warm, friendly, queer-owned.
  • Rosie's Bar & Grill — Brunch institution. American comfort food, drag brunch on weekends.
  • Tropics Grille — Casual American, live piano many evenings, large open patio.
  • Java Boys — The Drive's queer-owned coffeehouse.

Greater Fort Lauderdale — Beyond Wilton Drive

Sebastian Beach

The unofficial gay beach of Greater Fort Lauderdale sits just south of the main Fort Lauderdale Beach strip, accessed via Sebastian Street. From Paradise Vacation Suites, it's about an 18-minute drive (4.2 miles) via Sunrise Boulevard. Free parking is challenging on weekends — rideshare is usually easier.

Las Olas Boulevard

Downtown Fort Lauderdale's premier dining-and-shopping street, about a 13-minute drive south. Welcoming and walkable, with several gay-popular restaurants.

The Pride Center at Equality Park

The community hub of LGBTQ+ Greater Fort Lauderdale — programming, library, gallery, support services. Located on the southern end of Wilton Manors at NE 26th Street. Worth a stop if you're interested in the area's history.

Stonewall National Museum & Archives

One of the largest LGBTQ+ museum and archive institutions in the country, located in Wilton Manors. Open to the public Wednesday through Saturday.

Safety, Welcome & What to Expect

Wilton Manors is one of the safest and most genuinely welcoming places in the United States to travel as an LGBTQ+ visitor — singly, as a couple, or as a group. Public displays of affection are unremarkable. Pride flags fly from city hall, businesses, and residences year-round. Hand-holding and casual affection on the Drive are not just tolerated — they're the default.

The broader Fort Lauderdale area is also welcoming, though slightly less concentrated. Some areas of Broward and Palm Beach counties west of I-95 are more conservative; for visitors who care, sticking to Wilton Manors, downtown Fort Lauderdale, Las Olas, and the beach corridor is the safest bet.
