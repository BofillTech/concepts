A Visitor's Guide

The Wilton Manors Guide

Everything you need to know about South Florida's Island City — where to eat, where to drink, where to dance, when to come, and how to get the most out of three days, a week, or a month.

TL;DR

Wilton Manors is a 1.95-square-mile city of about 12,000 people in Broward County, completely surrounded by the city of Fort Lauderdale and ringed by the Middle and East branches of the Middle River — earning it the nickname "the Island City." Its cultural spine is Wilton Drive, a celebrated mile of restaurants, cafés, bars, galleries, and nightlife. It's the most walkable square mile in South Florida and one of the most genuinely LGBTQ+-welcoming places in the country.

The Island City — Geography & History

Wilton Manors sits in central Broward County, just north of downtown Fort Lauderdale. The city covers 1.95 square miles, with a population of around 12,000 residents. It is geographically bordered on three sides by branches of the Middle River — the Middle Fork to the south and west, the East Fork to the north and east — which is where the "Island City" name comes from.

The city was incorporated in 1947 as a quiet residential suburb. The transformation into the South Florida cultural capital it is today began in the 1990s, when Wilton Drive — the commercial main street running north-south through the city — began attracting LGBTQ+-owned businesses and residents. By the early 2000s, Wilton Manors had become a recognized destination, and by the 2020 US Census, the city had the second-highest concentration of same-sex couples of any city in the United States, after only Provincetown, Massachusetts.

Key Fact
Wilton Manors is one of just six US cities ever certified by Human Rights Campaign with a perfect Municipal Equality Index score of 100/100 — a position the city has held continuously since 2013.

Wilton Drive — The Cultural Spine

Most of what you'll do in Wilton Manors happens on or just off Wilton Drive, a roughly one-mile commercial corridor running from Five Points (the intersection at NE 26th Street, the city's southern gateway) up to Northeast 21st Court. The Drive is flat, tree-lined, and walkable — and from Paradise Vacation Suites at 2120 NE 26th Street, you're a 10-minute walk to the heart of it.

Where to Eat

Wilton Drive has an outsized restaurant scene for a one-mile street. A few to know:

  • Tulio's Tacos & Tequila — Mexican-American with one of the best tequila lists in South Florida. Generous portions, lively patio. Approximately 12-minute walk from Paradise.
  • Rosie's Bar & Grill — A Wilton Drive institution. American comfort food, drag brunch on weekends, world-class people-watching. About 22-minute walk.
  • Tropics Grille — Casual American with live piano many nights. Big covered patio facing the Drive.
  • EHRU — Plant-based, beautifully presented. The cocktails are taken seriously.
  • Java Boys — The Drive's coffee spot. Local, queer-owned, and where you'll meet half the neighborhood before noon.
  • Pizza Loft — A neighborhood pizzeria that has been around long enough to be considered an institution.

Where to Drink

The bars along the Drive cover everything from quiet wine moments to full-throttle dance floors.

  • The Manor Complex — A multi-room nightlife venue that includes The Manor (main dance floor), Eden (lounge), and Honey (cocktail bar). About 19-minute walk.
  • Hunters Nightclub — Big crowds, big music, drag shows nightly. The largest dance venue on the Drive. About 15-minute walk.
  • Georgie's Alibi Monkey Bar — Long-running neighborhood bar, multiple rooms, karaoke and trivia nights.
  • The New Moon — Lesbian bar, queer-owned, all genders welcome. Small, warm, and famously friendly.

Where to Shop

  • Pride Center at Equality Park — Not shopping per se, but the Drive's most important community space. The library, the bookstore, and event programming are all worth a look.
  • Wilton Drive vintage and gift shops — Several small independent retailers carry everything from coastal art to local-designer jewelry to vintage barware.

The Beach, Las Olas & Beyond

While Wilton Manors itself is inland, you're closer to the major Fort Lauderdale attractions than most people realize.

  • Fort Lauderdale Beach — 4.2 miles, approximately 18-minute drive via Sunrise Boulevard.
  • Sebastian Beach — The unofficial gay beach, just south of the main strip. Same drive time.
  • Las Olas Boulevard — Downtown Fort Lauderdale's premier dining and shopping street. About 13-minute drive.
  • Riverwalk — Downtown's mile-long waterfront promenade, with restaurants and the Broward Center for the Performing Arts. About 14 minutes.
  • Hollywood Beach Boardwalk — A more old-Florida boardwalk feel, 25 minutes south.
  • Miami / South Beach — 35-45 minutes south depending on traffic.

Getting Here & Getting Around

From the airport

Most visitors fly into Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL), which is exactly 4 miles south of Wilton Manors — about a 10-minute drive. Rideshare (Uber, Lyft) from FLL to Paradise typically runs $15-22.

Miami International Airport (MIA) is a 45-minute drive south via I-95. Cheaper flights sometimes make MIA worthwhile, but the time and rideshare cost ($50-70) usually offset the savings.

Getting around once you're here

Three modes work best:

  1. Walking. Wilton Drive and almost everything you'll want to do is walkable from anywhere central in the city. From Paradise, the Drive is 10 minutes on foot.
  2. Rideshare. Uber and Lyft are abundant. Most trips inside Greater Fort Lauderdale run $10-18.
  3. Rental car. Worth it if you plan to explore the Everglades, the Keys, or the Palm Beaches. Less essential for a Wilton Manors-focused stay.

When to Visit

South Florida runs on two seasons: high season (November through April) and low season (May through October).

High Season — November through April

The weather is in the 70s-low 80s, humidity is bearable, and the snowbirds are here. Restaurants are at full capacity, the Drive is buzzing, and rates are at their highest. Book 30-60 days ahead for the best selection.

Low Season — May through October

Hotter and more humid, but the locals will tell you the secret: the rain is short and predictable, the pool stays open, the restaurants are easier to walk into, and the rates drop significantly. Hurricane season runs June through November, with peak risk August-October.

Special Events Worth Planning Around

  • Stonewall Pride Wilton Manors — Mid-June. South Florida's largest Pride event, drawing 50,000+ to a parade on Wilton Drive. Book six months ahead.
  • Pride House Wilton Manors — Spring. Local Pride community festival.
  • Fort Lauderdale Boat Show — Late October / early November. The world's largest in-water boat show — drives strong demand citywide.
  • Art Fort Lauderdale — Late January / early February.
  • Las Olas Wine & Food Festival — April.
