Our Story · Est. 1992

From the granite walls of Yosemite to the canopy of Coconino.

Three decades of climbing rope, chainsaw oil, and the same family name on the door. We didn’t come to this trade through a classroom — we came to it the long way, on rope, off rock, and into the pines.

01 Jim’s Chapter — 1980s & 1990s

A climber, by trade.

In the late 1980s, Jim Gaun was a notorious rock climber on the high walls of Yosemite. El Capitan and the surrounding granite were his proving ground. The climbing community knew him by name.

When word came that those same skills could put food on the table — that a man with a family to support could trade rock for bark and make a living — Jim founded Gaun’s Tree Service. The year was 1992. The truck and the rope were the same. Only the medium changed.

His workmanship was such that the National Park Service once filmed him as a teaching example — using his climbing and rigging technique as the standard for proper removal and pruning. It is one of the proudest moments in the company’s history, and the standard he set on those tapes is the standard the company still works to.

Jim passed in 2021. His son has run the company since.

02 Jordan’s Chapter — 2005 to Present

Twenty years on rope.

Jordan Gaun joined his father on the truck at sixteen. He has now been in the trees for twenty years. Everything he knows about climbing, rigging, and reading a tree he learned with Jim’s hand on his shoulder and a 200-foot canopy overhead.

When Jim passed in 2021, Jordan took over the company. With his father’s training as the foundation, he has grown it considerably — and continues to learn as new techniques and care-taking practices for trees evolve. The standard hasn’t changed. The work has just gotten bigger.

Where Jordan shines is removal, rigging, and reading risk. Advanced climbing and technical removals — the trees that other crews look at and pass on. The trees that need a man on rope, a crane on standby, and a foreman with thirty years of judgment between them.

“ The best rigger, climber, and most efficient arborist in Northern Arizona. Same rope work my father taught me — same standard, same town. — Jordan Gaun
