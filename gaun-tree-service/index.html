What we do

Five ways we work the land.

Every property is different. Every tree is different. We assess, we plan, and we leave your land cleaner, safer, and healthier than we found it.

I

Hazardous Tree Removal

Practiced arborists assess dead, diseased and structurally compromised trees. Advanced climbing & rigging, cranes and man lifts when the tree demands it, modern safety protocols, and a full clean-up — including stump grinding and debris haul — when the work is done.

  • Expert assessment
  • Specialized rigging & equipment
  • Stump grinding & debris removal
  • 24/7 emergency response
II

Pruning

Structural and aesthetic pruning that keeps trees healthy, beautiful, and out of trouble. We work with the tree, not against it — protecting long-term form, light, and the resources around it.

  • Structural pruning
  • Crown thinning & raising
  • Deadwood removal
  • Aesthetic shaping
III

Thinning & Firewise

Wildfire risk doesn't wait. We assess your property for fire hazards and implement strategic thinning — buffer zones, defensible space, and a healthier forest that resists ignition and spread.

  • Wildfire risk reduction
  • Defensible space planning
  • Strategic tree spacing
  • Ongoing maintenance plans
IV

Lot Clearing & Prep

Residential or commercial — we clear trees, brush, debris and unwanted vegetation so you can build. Eco-friendly practices, advanced equipment, and a property ready for its next chapter.

  • Residential & commercial lots
  • Brush, debris & vegetation
  • Recycling where possible
  • Timely turnarounds

A family business

From Yosemite walls to the Coconino canopy.

In the late 1980s, Jim Gaun was a notorious rock climber on the high walls of Yosemite — El Capitan and the surrounding granite. When word came that those same skills could put food on the table, he traded rock for bark and founded Gaun’s Tree Service in 1993. His workmanship was such that the National Park Service once filmed him as a teaching example for proper removal and pruning technique.

Jordan Gaun joined his father on the truck at sixteen and has now been in the trees for twenty years. Jim passed in 2021. Jordan has run the company since — alongside a right-hand man with three decades in the trade and a young crew his father’s standards shaped from the climbing line down.

“ Removal, rigging, and reading risk — that’s where we shine. Same rope work my father taught me, same standard, same town. — Jordan Gaun
