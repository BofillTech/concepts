On The Last Virgin Island

A little Italy on a quiet island.

Tucked into the corner of White Bay at the Beach Club, Alice's is the kind of place sailors moor up for and barefoot guests stay all afternoon. Caribbean breeze, an Italian-leaning kitchen, and a menu that moves with whatever came in off the boats and out of the gardens.

We source on-island first — even the wood for the oven is gathered locally — and the rest comes straight from Italy. It's a small place with a big point of view: come hungry, stay late, leave full.

The Oven

The only wood-fired oven in the BVI.

Built by hand and powered by locally-gathered wood, our oven runs hot enough to char a sourdough crust in ninety seconds and tender enough to cradle a two-pound lobster tail. Same fire, two very different stories.

It sits under solar panels that run the rest of the kitchen, so the meal you're eating leaves about as small a footprint as a meal can. Pizza for lunch, lobster for dinner, sustainability for everyone.
