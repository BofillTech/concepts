To the Lake One Block

Fine food and quaint lodgings are what Greunke's is known for in the Bayfield, Wisconsin area — and well beyond. Our twelve charming rooms occupy two of Bayfield's oldest historic structures, the LaBonte House and the Wachsmuth House, both quietly anchoring downtown for more than a century and a half.

Stepping into Greunke's is like stepping back into the '40s — the Coca‑Cola memorabilia, the working Wurlitzer, the old soda fountain counter — images straight from a Norman Rockwell cover. — A Note from the Inn

Centrally located, the Inn is a short walk from Lake Superior, the Madeline Island Ferry, the Apostle Islands Cruise Service, the town pool, the shops, and most of Bayfield's attractions. Shuttle service to Big Top Chautauqua is available for our guests.

Six rooms have private baths and queen beds. Two have queen beds with a half bath. Four rooms — reminiscent of the Inn's earliest days — have shared facilities. Each is comfortable, quiet, and decorated with antiques and original art.

Our Story

Two of Bayfield's oldest, most historic structures.

The First Street Inn is a careful combination of two of Bayfield's earliest buildings — the LaBonte House, built in 1863 by a young French‑Canadian who would later help convince the railroads to come to the peninsula, and the Wachsmuth House from 1880, once Whisker Wachsmuth's tobacco shop.

Today they're combined into a single small inn, listed in the directory of Historic Wisconsin Inns and Harvard's Let's Go Guide to the United States.

1863 LaBonte House welcomes its first guests, between the Hotel Bracken and Wachsmith's tobacco shop.
1880 The Wachsmuth House is built next door — its rooms later named for the Apostle Islands.
1940 Greunke's begins serving food to locals and visitors — the era our restaurant still channels today.
Today Twelve rooms, one beloved restaurant, and a working Wurlitzer that still plays.
