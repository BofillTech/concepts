America's Oldest Seaport

Why Gloucester.

America's oldest seaport. Closest port to two major whale feeding grounds. Home dock of The Perfect Storm.

  1. Home
  2. Why Gloucester

Gloucester is the oldest seaport in America — and the most direct route to one of the world's great whale watching destinations. Here's why sailing from Gloucester beats anywhere else on the East Coast.

Closest Port to Two Major Feeding Grounds

Gloucester sits closer than any other major port to two of the richest whale feeding grounds off the Massachusetts coast:

  • Stellwagen Bank — 12 miles east. A 638-square-mile federal marine sanctuary. World Wildlife Fund Top 5 whale watching destination on the planet.
  • Jeffreys Ledge — 25 miles northeast. A 33-mile undersea ridge that's a humpback feeding hotspot in late summer.

Boston whale watches have to travel almost twice as far to reach Stellwagen. Newburyport is similar distance but has a much smaller boat. Gloucester is the sweet spot.

The Hurricane II Advantage

Combine Gloucester's location with the Hurricane II's 33 mph cruise speed and you get to whales in about an hour. That means more time with the animals and less time on the ride.

America's Oldest Seaport

Gloucester was founded in 1623 — older than Boston (1630) and Salem (1626). 400 years of working waterfront. You can still see commercial fishing boats coming and going from the same harbor.

The Perfect Storm

Our home dock at Rose's Wharf is where the Andrea Gail and Hannah Boden tied up — the swordfishing boats made famous in Sebastian Junger's book and the 2000 movie. The Crow's Nest pub, where the crews drank, is directly across the street from our dock and still operates today.

Easy to Reach

Gloucester is 1 hour from Boston via I-93 N to MA-128. Take Exit 10. We're half a mile from the highway. From the South Shore, Cape Cod, Worcester, or western Mass, it's all under 3 hours.

What Else to Do in Gloucester

Eat Fresh Seafood

You're in the working fishing capital of America. The seafood is fresh. Try The Causeway, Topside Grill, Latitude 43, or The Studio. The fried clams and fish & chips from The Causeway are legendary.

Visit Rocky Neck Art Colony

One of the oldest continuously operating art colonies in America. Walk through working artists' studios, galleries, and harborfront restaurants.

The Fisherman's Memorial

The bronze fisherman statue gazing out to sea, with the inscription "They that go down to the sea in ships." A memorial to the 10,000+ Gloucester fishermen lost at sea since 1623. Quietly powerful.

Beaches

Good Harbor Beach and Wingaersheek Beach are two of the best beaches in New England. Soft sand, calm water, plenty of parking.

Hammond Castle

A medieval-style castle built by inventor John Hays Hammond Jr. in the 1920s. Right on the water. Tours daily in summer.

Cape Ann Museum

Gloucester's art and maritime history museum. Permanent exhibits on the working waterfront, Fitz Henry Lane's seascapes, and the history of fishing.

Make a Weekend of It

Check out our accommodations page for places to stay in Gloucester and nearby Rockport. Most of our passengers stay overnight and combine whale watching with beach days, restaurants, and Cape Ann exploration.
