A Letter from the Helm

We are the pioneers of whale watching in Gloucester.

Cape Ann Whale Watch has been carrying passengers out to Stellwagen Bank since 1979 — long before whale watching became a national pastime. My father started this company. Today my crew and I carry his legacy forward, leading guests to the same productive waters that draw humpbacks, fin whales, minkes, and dolphins back to Gloucester each year.

We go where the whales go. Our vessels stay in constant communication, tracking sightings across the North and South Shore. Wherever the whales are running — Stellwagen, Jeffreys Ledge, the waters off Provincetown — that's where we'll take you.

Please join us aboard.

Stellwagen Bank · 12 Miles Off Gloucester

What you'll see out there.

Stellwagen Bank is one of the most productive whale-feeding grounds on the Atlantic coast — abundant in herring and sand lance that draw whales back every spring. Here are the species our captains spot most often.

Megaptera novaeangliae

Humpback Whale

The headliner. Acrobatic, curious, and famously unafraid of boats — known for full-body breaches, tail slaps, and bubble-net feeding.

Balaenoptera physalus

Fin Whale

The second-largest animal on Earth, gliding through sanctuary waters at speeds up to 25 knots. Often spotted in summer feeding aggregations.

Balaenoptera acutorostrata

Minke Whale

The pocket-sized cousin — quick, curious, and a regular Stellwagen Bank visitor.

Lagenorhynchus acutus

Atlantic White-Sided Dolphin

Pods of dozens to hundreds, surfing the bow wake in dazzling formation.

Eubalaena glacialis

North Atlantic Right Whale

Among the rarest large whales on the planet — a privileged sighting when one passes through.

Phoca vitulina

Harbor Seal & Other Wildlife

Plus harbor porpoises, basking sharks, ocean sunfish, gannets, shearwaters, and the occasional sea turtle.

Globicephala melas

Pilot Whales, Sperm Whales & Sei Whales

The deepwater species. Less common, more spectacular when they appear off the rail.

Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary

Named one of the Top Five whale watching destinations on Earth.

Twelve miles off the Gloucester coast lies a submerged glacial bank that funnels nutrient-rich currents to the surface — drawing the largest seasonal concentration of feeding whales on the eastern seaboard. The World Wildlife Fund, USA Today, and Lonely Planet have all named it among the world's premier whale grounds.

— World Wildlife Fund
