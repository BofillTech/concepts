Education & Research

Educating globally.

Naturalists on every voyage. Hands-on teaching tools. World Cetacean Alliance partner. Stellwagen Bank research contributor.

  1. Home
  2. Education & Research

Cape Ann Whale Watch is one of the most education-focused whale watch companies in New England. We have an Education Coordinator on every voyage, hands-on teaching tools, and an active partnership with the World Cetacean Alliance.

"In the end, we will conserve only what we love, love only what we understand, and understand only what we are taught."
— Baba Dioum

Naturalists on Every Voyage

Every trip has a trained naturalist on board — most often Head Naturalist Tina McMahon or Education Coordinator Jamie McWilliams. They interact with passengers throughout the trip, answer questions, identify individual whales by their flukes, and immerse you in what you're seeing.

Tina McMahon · Head Naturalist

Tina has been with Cape Ann Whale Watch since the early 1990s. She is a PolarTREC teacher who participated in a research expedition to Greenland, and a member of the Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary Advisory Council. She knows the regular humpbacks by name.

Jamie McWilliams · Education Coordinator

Jamie's passion for whales and teaching is contagious. She leads the hands-on educational programs onboard and is repeatedly mentioned by name in TripAdvisor reviews:

"Jamie, the onboard naturalist, was extremely knowledgeable and passionate about her work."
— Sarah E., Richmond VA · TripAdvisor

Hands-On Teaching Tools

Our educators bring whales to life with all your senses:

  • Smell the baleen — the filter-feeding mechanism inside a humpback's mouth
  • Hear the whale songs — recordings of male humpback song from the breeding grounds
  • See a 3-D model of the ocean floor — understand why Stellwagen Bank is a feeding hotspot
  • Feel whale teeth — sperm whale teeth, baleen plates, and bone
  • Identify individuals — match flukes to our catalog of returning whales

Great for Children

Children are encouraged to participate in everything. Our naturalists adjust their delivery to engage all ages — from preschoolers to teens. Multiple TripAdvisor reviews mention how children walk off the boat fascinated, ready to learn more.

"The education staff worked hard to educate us about what we were seeing and certainly stimulated the curiosity of my seven-year-old granddaughter."
— ImOma, Titusville FL · TripAdvisor

World Cetacean Alliance Partner

Cape Ann Whale Watch is proud to be a partner with the World Cetacean Alliance (WCA), the largest marine conservation partnership in the world. WCA works across the globe with tourism businesses, scientists, and artists on a united mission to protect whales and dolphins. WCA also leads the global movement for responsible whale watching tourism — making sure the industry benefits whales and dolphins, not just operators.

Stellwagen Bank Research

We share our daily sightings, photo IDs, and behavior observations with researchers at the Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary. Our fluke catalog contributes to ongoing studies of humpback whale populations in the Gulf of Maine. Tina sits on the Sanctuary's Advisory Council.

School Field Trips

We work with K–12 schools, summer programs, college courses, and homeschool groups. Group rates available for parties of 20+. Pre-trip curriculum materials available on request. Call 1-800-877-5110 to book a school group.
