Chapter 01 — Welcome

Where mind, body & soul become one with the sea.

A name that goes back to the 1950s, when casino meant a meeting place — and Misquamicut’s was right here on the dunes.

Today the Atlantic Beach Casino Resort is a community of twenty-one privately owned, oceanfront condominium suites set on a quiet stretch of Atlantic Avenue between Misquamicut and Watch Hill. The original Atlantic Beach Casino stood next door, where the Windjammer is now. We carry the name forward — and the spirit of a place built simply to gather, swim, and watch the Atlantic.

You wake to the waves crashing. You watch the sun rise over the ocean. You walk for miles in either direction without leaving the dunes. Saturday-to-Saturday, all summer long, generations of families have called this their slice of heaven on Earth.

Chapter 03 — The Setting

On the dunes, between the Sound and the open sea.

Atlantic Beach Casino Resort sits along the Westerly stretch of the Rhode Island shore, where the dunes give way to miles of open sand in either direction and the coastal islands sit on the horizon. Ours is the building closest to the water — at high tide, the surf runs beneath us.

Down the beach are restaurants, ice cream stands, and the Misquamicut boardwalk. Across the street: a saltwater pond, mini golf, batting cages, and Water Wizz waterslides. Fifteen minutes east are Mystic Seaport and Foxwoods. Fifteen minutes west are the lights of New London. And the rest is salt and silence.

Chapter 06 — Westerly

The town that isn’t the Hamptons.

Westerly is a working coastal town — less polished than Newport, less precious than Martha’s Vineyard, more lived-in than Block Island. It has a downtown with a small cinema and a 200-voice chorus, two state beaches, the Watch Hill lighthouse, and a Taylor Swift sighting if you’re lucky.
